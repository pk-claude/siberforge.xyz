How is the US consumer + housing actually doing?

Macro regimes are an averaging story. This page is bottom-up: spending power, balance-sheet stress, housing-cycle position, and the channels that flow into home-improvement & building-materials demand (HD/LOW/XHB/WOOD + lumber PPI). The intent is decision-relevant for anyone whose P&L correlates with the housing cycle. Related: regime view, cycle position, inflation persistence.

1. Housing cycle — where are we on the wheel?

Permits lead starts by 1–2 months; starts lead completions by 6–9 months; new home sales lead resales by 6–12 months. Months Supply (new homes / monthly sales rate) is the single cleanest cycle-position read — < 4 = sellers’ market, 4–6 = balanced, > 6 = buyers’ market / inventory glut. The 30Y mortgage rate drives all of this with a 3–6 month lag.

2. Home improvement & building materials — channel check

Home Depot and Lowe’s capture ~50% of the US home-improvement market between them. Their relative performance vs SPY tells you whether the home-improvement cycle is leading, lagging, or in line with the broader market. Lumber PPI is a leading indicator of pro-vs-DIY mix and revenue dynamics for both retailers and lumber distributors. WOOD ETF tracks publicly traded lumber/forestry; XHB tracks homebuilders.

3. Consumer balance sheet & spending

Three layers: income (real disposable), cushion (savings rate + debt service ratio), stress (credit-card delinquency). Consumption is ~70% of US GDP. When real wages turn negative or delinquencies spike, the consumer-led cycle is rolling over — usually 2–3 quarters before it shows up in retail.
